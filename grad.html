Advanced General Relativity and Perturbation Theory.

General Relativity

General Relativity (GR) describes gravity not as a force, but as the curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy.

Einstein Field Equations

The core of GR is a set of ten nonlinear partial differential equations:

$$ R_{\mu\nu} - \frac{1}{2}Rg_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4} T_{\mu\nu} $$
Tensor Components
  • $R_{\mu\nu}$: Ricci Curvature Tensor
  • $R$: Ricci Scalar
  • $g_{\mu\nu}$: Metric Tensor
  • $\Lambda$: Cosmological Constant
  • $T_{\mu\nu}$: Stress-Energy Tensor

The Schwarzschild Metric

The first exact solution to the EFE, describing the geometry outside a spherical, non-rotating mass:

$$ ds^2 = -\left(1-\frac{r_s}{r}\right)c^2dt^2 + \left(1-\frac{r_s}{r}\right)^{-1}dr^2 + r^2d\Omega^2 $$

Where $r_s = \frac{2GM}{c^2}$ is the Schwarzschild radius.

Perturbation Theory in Astrodynamics

Real orbits are never perfectly Keplerian due to non-spherical central bodies, atmospheric drag, and third-body effects. We use the Lagrange Planetary Equations to model these variations.

J2 Perturbation

The Earth's oblateness (J2 term) causes the regression of the ascending node ($\Omega$) and the precession of the argument of perigee ($\omega$):

$$ \dot{\Omega} = -\frac{3}{2} J_2 \left(\frac{R_E}{p}\right)^2 n \cos i $$ $$ \dot{\omega} = \frac{3}{4} J_2 \left(\frac{R_E}{p}\right)^2 n (5 \cos^2 i - 1) $$

Stanford AA 279 Track

The gold standard in modern astrodynamics.

  • AA 279A/B/C Space Mechanics:
    • 279A: Orbits, J2 perturbation, Gauss's Variational Equations.
    • 279B: Interplanetary navigation and Orbit Determination (Kalman Filtering).
    • 279C: Optimal Control for low-thrust trajectories (ion engines).
  • AA 203 Optimal and Learning-Based Control: Blends classical optimal control (Pontryagin's Minimum Principle) with modern Reinforcement Learning.

Part 1: The Core Curriculum (The "Big Three")

A comparative look at how the giants of aerospace education approach the fundamentals.

1. MIT (Course 16: AeroAstro)

Focus: The "Unified Engineering" approach—teaching fluids, structures, and signals simultaneously.

  • 16.001 – 16.004 Unified Engineering I-IV:
    • Materials & Structures: Statics, trusses, stress-strain, beam bending, failure modes.
    • Signals & Systems: Linear systems, Fourier/Laplace transforms, feedback control.
    • Fluid Dynamics: Conservation laws, compressible flow, shock waves, wing aerodynamics.
    • Thermodynamics & Propulsion: Brayton cycles, heat transfer, jet engine performance.
  • 16.07 Dynamics: Particle dynamics, orbital mechanics, rigid body kinematics.
  • 16.410 Principles of Autonomy: Reason-based AI, path planning (A*), constraint satisfaction.

2. Stanford University (Aero/Astro Major)

Focus: Flexible "Tracks" allowing early specialization in Space vs. Aviation.

  • AA 100 Intro to Aeronautics and Astronautics: Physics of flight and space survey.
  • AA 102 Applied Aerodynamics: Airfoil theory, compressibility, viscous flow.
  • AA 103 Air and Space Propulsion: Rocket equation, nozzle design, turbomachinery.
  • AA 279A Space Mechanics: Orbits, J2 perturbation, Gauss's variational equations.
  • AA 115Q The Global Positioning System: GPS signal processing and triangulation.
  • AA 120Q Building Trust in Autonomy: Safety-critical AI and human-robot interaction.

3. Caltech (Aerospace Minor/Focus)

Focus: Physics-first, highly theoretical and mathematical rigor.

  • Ae 101 Fluid Mechanics: Navier-Stokes equations, vorticity, boundary layers.
  • Ae 102 Solid Mechanics: Elasticity, plasticity, wave propagation.
  • Ae 103 Aerospace Control Systems: State-space control, stability analysis, robust control.
  • Ae 105 Space Engineering: Mission design, astrodynamics, spacecraft subsystems.
  • Ae 121 Space Propulsion: Electric propulsion (Hall thrusters), chemical rockets, plasma physics.

Part 2: Deep Dive & Open Source Labs

Match the curriculum topics with the best open-source repositories.

A. Astrodynamics & Orbit Design

Curriculum Match: MIT 16.07, Stanford AA 279A, Caltech Ae 105

  • The "Gold Standard" Repo: alfonsogonzalez/AWP
    Description: "Astrodynamics with Python". Covers Keplerian orbits, J2 perturbation, interplanetary transfers.

B. Navigation & GPS

Curriculum Match: Stanford AA 115Q, AA 272

  • The "Stanford" Repo: Stanford-NavLab/gnss_lib_py
    Description: Official tool from Stanford's NavLab. Parses GNSS data, calculates state estimates.

C. Control Systems & Autonomy

Curriculum Match: MIT 16.30, Caltech Ae 103

  • The "Implementation" Repo: python-control/python-control
    Description: Standard Python library for feedback control systems (Bode plots, State-Space analysis).

D. Unified Engineering

Curriculum Match: MIT 16.001–16.004

  • The "Resource" Repo: MIT OpenCourseWare
    Description: Problem sets and lecture notes for the legendary Unified Engineering course.

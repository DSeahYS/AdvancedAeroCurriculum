Foundational concepts in Astrodynamics and Relativity.

Orbital Mechanics

At the undergraduate level, we move beyond simple Keplerian orbits to understand the dynamics of spacecraft maneuvering.

The Vis-Viva Equation

The fundamental equation governing the motion of orbiting bodies is the Vis-Viva equation, derived from the conservation of energy:

$$ v^2 = GM \left( \frac{2}{r} - \frac{1}{a} \right) $$

Where $v$ is the relative speed, $r$ is the distance between the two bodies, $a$ is the semi-major axis, $G$ is the gravitational constant, and $M$ is the mass of the central body.

Hohmann Transfer

The most energy-efficient transfer between two coplanar circular orbits is the Hohmann transfer ellipse. The delta-v required is the sum of two impulsive burns:

$$ \Delta v_{total} = \Delta v_1 + \Delta v_2 $$ $$ \Delta v_1 = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r_1}} \left( \sqrt{\frac{2r_2}{r_1+r_2}} - 1 \right) $$

Special Relativity

Introduced by Einstein in 1905, Special Relativity fundamentally changed our understanding of space and time. The invariant interval $ds^2$ is given by:

$$ ds^2 = -c^2dt^2 + dx^2 + dy^2 + dz^2 = \eta_{\mu\nu} dx^\mu dx^\nu $$

Where $\eta_{\mu\nu} = \text{diag}(-1, 1, 1, 1)$ is the Minkowski metric. This leads to time dilation and length contraction.
